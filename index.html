THE IDEA.

Forage Meets Hunt was born from the inspiration of top rated grocers who are dedicated to supplying fresh and top quality produce. They also select specific foods to target a certain audience. Forage and Hunt was designed as a concept store for people who cater to a "paleo lifestyle", potentially selling produce that comes under the realm of a paleolithic diet such as meats, fish, regional veggies,leafy greens, nuts and seeds.

THE INSPIRATION.

Stores like Wilder & Hunt, Little Bird and Little and Friday commit themselves to selling produce that is on trend with healthy lifestyle eating such as being gluten free, soy free etc. The inspiration was found by their branding concepts of using archaic-like symbols such as Wilder and Hunt's arrow, nature like Little Bird's bird mascot and a name that hints a story of their background. Simplifying nature through basic symbols gave the idea for the simplistic design of Forage Meets Hunt.

THE LOGO.

The name "Forage meets Hunt" derived from the idea of archaic human practices which in a historical context, it was a necessity to forage and hunt for freshly grown and local produce. To keep to the theme, the arrow and the leaf were purposefully kept simple to portray paleolithic cave drawings, where "The hunt and the gather" were stories told through drawings created by the early homo sapiens. The black circle symbolises the 'serving' of a meal, such as on a platter or plate.

THE TYPOGRAPHY.

The text for the logo is 'Georgia Regular'. The original text size for the F and H is 600pt while the first letters of the words 'Forage' and 'Hunt' are 50pt. The rest of the letters in the Forage meets Hunt sentence are 42pt. If upscaled/downscaled, these ratios in the sizes need to be at an approximate scale.

THE COLOR.

Black and white are the colour contrasts used for Forage meets Hunt. The hexcode approved for the black is #131313 while the off-white hexcode is #F5F5F5. Both can be used depending on which one suits the choosen background.
